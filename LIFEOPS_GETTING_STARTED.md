## What is Life Operations?

Think of Life Operations as your personal life assistant - it watches, learns, and helps you understand yourself better.

### The Simple Version

Life Operations does three things:

1. **Tracks your life** - Sleep, activity, health, and daily routines
2. **Finds patterns** - Discovers what helps or hurts your wellbeing
3. **Gives insights** - Tells you what it all means in simple English

### Why You'd Want This

**Have you ever wondered...**

- "Why am I always tired on Mondays?"
- "When am I most productive?"
- "Does my sleep really affect my mood?"
- "Am I getting enough exercise?"
- "What's causing my stress?"

Life Operations answers these questions automatically. No spreadsheets, no manual tracking - it just works.

## How to Get Started

### Step 1: Set Up Your Data Sources

Life Operations can track your life from multiple sources. Pick what works for you:

#### Option 1: Fitbit (Recommended)
**What you need:** A Fitbit device

**What you get:** Automatic tracking of sleep, steps, heart rate, and activity

**Setup time:** 5 minutes

**How to connect:**
1. Open Life Operations
2. Go to Settings ‚Üí Connections
3. Click "Connect Fitbit"
4. Log in to your Fitbit account
5. Done! Data starts flowing automatically

#### Option 2: Smart Home Cameras
**What you need:** Home security cameras (Roku, Ring, etc.)

**What you get:** Activity tracking at home, safety monitoring, routine detection

**Setup time:** 10 minutes

**How to connect:**
1. Make sure cameras are on your home network
2. Open Life Operations
3. Go to Settings ‚Üí Cameras
4. Add camera (it will scan your network)
5. Choose what to monitor (privacy-friendly - no recordings stored)

#### Option 3: Manual Tracking
**What you need:** Just your phone or computer

**What you get:** Track anything you want, whenever you want

**Setup time:** Immediate

**How to use:**
1. Open Life Operations
2. Click "Add Event"
3. Select what happened (sleep, exercise, meal, etc.)
4. Add details
5. Save

### Step 2: Let It Learn

For the first week, Life Operations is learning about you:

**Day 1-3:** Basic data collection  
**Day 4-7:** Starts finding simple patterns  
**Week 2+:** Deep insights and recommendations  

**Tip:** The more data sources you connect, the better insights you get!

### Step 3: Start Asking Questions

After a few days of data, you can start asking questions:

**Try these first:**
- "How did I sleep this week?"
- "Show me my activity patterns"
- "What did I do yesterday?"

Then get more specific:
- "Why am I tired today?"
- "When am I most productive?"
- "Am I getting enough exercise?"

## Understanding Your Insights

### What Are Patterns?

Patterns are connections Life Operations finds in your data. There are four types:

#### 1. Habits (Things You Do Regularly)
**Example:** "You wake up at 7:15am ¬±20 minutes every weekday"

**Why it matters:** Understanding your habits helps you work with them, not against them.

**What you can do:** Schedule important tasks around your natural habits.

#### 2. Correlations (Things That Connect)
**Example:** "When you exercise in the morning, you sleep 30% better that night"

**Why it matters:** Shows cause and effect in your life.

**What you can do:** Do more of what helps, less of what hurts.

#### 3. Trends (Things Changing Over Time)
**Example:** "Your activity has decreased 15% over the last month"

**Why it matters:** Catch issues before they become problems.

**What you can do:** Course-correct early.

#### 4. Anomalies (Unusual Events)
**Example:** "You only slept 4 hours last night - that's 3 hours below your average"

**Why it matters:** Highlights things that need attention.

**What you can do:** Investigate and address unusual events.

### Confidence Levels

Each insight has a confidence level:

**High Confidence (‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ)**
- Based on lots of data
- Consistent pattern
- Very likely accurate
- Act on these

**Medium Confidence (‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÜ‚òÜ)**
- Based on some data
- Pattern emerging
- Probably accurate
- Worth watching

**Low Confidence (‚òÖ‚òÜ‚òÜ‚òÜ‚òÜ)**
- Limited data
- Possible pattern
- Might be random
- Don't act yet, just note it

## Common Questions

### "How much do I need to track?"

**Minimum:** Sleep (7+ nights)  
**Good:** Sleep + Activity (2 weeks)  
**Best:** Multiple sources (1 month)

The more data, the better insights. But you'll start seeing value after just one week of basic tracking.

### "Is my data private?"

**Yes.** Your data stays on your device/local network. Nothing is shared unless you explicitly choose to.

**What's stored locally:**
- All your life events
- Patterns discovered
- Your queries and results

**What uses cloud services (optional):**
- AI analysis (uses OpenAI/Google, you can disable)
- Cloud backup (optional)

**You control:**
- What's tracked
- What's analyzed
- What's stored
- What's shared

### "What if I miss a day?"

**No problem!** Life Operations works with whatever data you have. Missing days just mean:
- Fewer insights for that period
- No impact on other patterns
- Can always fill in manually if you want

### "Can I track custom things?"

**Absolutely!** Track anything:

**Examples:**
- Medication taken
- Mood ratings
- Meals and diet
- Work sessions
- Meditation
- Symptoms
- Anything else

**How:**
1. Open Sophia
2. Click "Add Event"
3. Choose "Custom"
4. Name it and add details
5. Life Operations starts tracking it

### "Will it nag me?"

**Only if you want it to.**

Default mode: Gentle reminders and helpful insights

**You can adjust:**
- Frequency (daily, weekly, never)
- Urgency (only important vs. everything)
- Type (health only, patterns only, everything)
- Timing (morning, evening, don't disturb hours)

**Settings ‚Üí Notifications ‚Üí Customize**

### "What if I don't like an insight?"

**Three options:**

1. **Dismiss it** - "Not helpful" button
2. **Provide feedback** - Tell Sophia why it's wrong
3. **Adjust sensitivity** - Settings ‚Üí Insights ‚Üí Threshold

Life Operations learns what you find helpful and adjusts.

### "Can multiple people use it?"

**Yes!** Each person gets their own profile.

**How it works:**
- Each person logs in with their account
- Data is completely separate
- No cross-contamination
- Privacy maintained

**Great for:**
- Couples tracking together
- Family health monitoring
- Roommate house management

## Real Life Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Better Sleep

**Problem:** Sarah feels tired all the time.

**What she does:**
1. Connects her Fitbit to Life Operations
2. Tracks sleep for 2 weeks
3. Asks: "Why am I always tired?"

**What Sophia finds:**
- Sarah averages only 6.2 hours sleep
- She goes to bed at different times (10pm-1am)
- Best sleep happens when she's in bed by 10:30pm
- She sleeps worse after drinking coffee after 3pm

**Sarah's changes:**
- Sets 10pm bedtime routine
- Stops afternoon coffee
- Keeps consistent sleep schedule

**Result:** After 3 weeks, Sarah averages 7.5 hours and feels much better. Life Operations confirmed the improvement with data.

### Scenario 2: Managing Stress

**Problem:** Mike feels stressed but doesn't know why.

**What he does:**
1. Lets Life Operations monitor his heart rate
2. Manually logs when he feels stressed
3. Tracks work sessions
4. Asks: "What's causing my stress?"

**What Sophia finds:**
- Heart rate spikes during afternoon meetings
- Stress correlates with skipping lunch
- Lowest stress on days with morning exercise
- Stress builds up when he doesn't take breaks

**Mike's changes:**
- Blocks lunch time in calendar
- Exercises 3 mornings per week
- Takes 10-minute breaks every 90 minutes
- Prepares for afternoon meetings

**Result:** Stress reduced by ~40% (measured by heart rate variability). Mike now knows his triggers and how to manage them.

### Scenario 3: Improving Fitness

**Problem:** Emma wants to exercise more but keeps failing.

**What she does:**
1. Sets goal: Exercise 4x per week
2. Tracks attempts and successes
3. Asks: "When should I exercise?"

**What Sophia finds:**
- Emma has 90% success rate with morning exercise
- Only 20% success rate with evening exercise
- Most successful on Monday/Wednesday/Friday
- Works best after light breakfast

**Emma's changes:**
- Schedules exercise for 7am M/W/F
- Prepares gym bag night before
- Eats banana before workout
- Stops planning evening workouts

**Result:** Went from exercising 1x per week to 3-4x per week consistently. Life Operations helped her work WITH her natural patterns.

### Scenario 4: Understanding Productivity

**Problem:** James can't figure out why some days are productive and others aren't.

**What he does:**
1. Manually logs productive vs. unproductive work sessions
2. Tracks sleep, meals, and breaks
3. Asks: "What makes me productive?"

**What Sophia finds:**
- Best productivity: 10am-12pm (peak focus window)
- Good sleep = 50% more productive next day
- Productivity drops 70% after lunch (food coma)
- Short walks boost focus for 2 hours after

**James's changes:**
- Does important work 10am-12pm
- Prioritizes sleep (7.5+ hours)
- Takes 15-minute walk after lunch
- Saves routine tasks for afternoon slump

**Result:** Accomplishes more in 4 focused hours than previous 8-hour days. Life Operations showed him when to work, not just to work more.
